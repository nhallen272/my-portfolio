About Me

I am currently a Computer Science student at Old Dominion University. I received an Associate of Science with a specialization in Computer Science from Tidewater Community College in 2021 and have chosen to pursue a bachelor's in Computer Science from ODU. A designer at heart, I live to build and engineer things. I have a background working in many types of jobs and I prefer a diverse work environment. My Skills and Experiences

Since I started at TCC in 2019, with the goal of building a career, I have actively sought to improve my programming experience and skill both in and outside of the classroom. In school I learned C++, but meanwhile, I was also learning Python, embedded systems and IoT platforms such as ESP32, Thingsboard, and Arduino. In 2020, I collaborated with colleagues at ODU and co-authored a paper about the pandemic. During the height of the pandemic, we analyed webcam data using GIS tools to quantify social-distancing and to answer the question: "Is it safer at the beach?" My role was data acquisition and formatting; I wrote the web scraper that periodically captured video from a live webcam stream, then captured frames from the video and saved them. I am currently available for hire for tasks that require coding such as web scraping, testing with Selenium, automation and scripting.


Currently, I work for the ODU Computer Science Systems Group. This is a student-run IT team that upkeeps the backend and network of the CS department.
